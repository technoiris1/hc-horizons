# FAQ

## What is Horizons?
Horizons is a series of hackathons happening in San Francisco, Singapore, Central Europe, South America, Australia/New Zealand, and Africa. Horizons Nexus (the event in San Francisco) is on June 19-21st! (Dates and exact cities are TBA for the other events.)

## What's a hackathon?

A hackathon is an event where you build a technical project from scratch. It's about teamwork, creativity, having fun, and learning new things.

## Who can participate?

This event is open to anyone 13-18, regardless of skill level.

## All this for free? Is this legit?

Yup, all for free! [Hack Club is a 501(c)3 non-profit organization](https://the.hackfoundation.org/), and we've run a bunch of events like this in the past. For example, we ran a [180-person hardware hackathon at GitHub HQ](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kaEFv7e49mo), and a [seven-day game jam in Shanghai, China](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Gtjyyu82pw4).

## What if my parents are concerned?

We're here to help! Feel free to reach out to [horizons@hackclub.com](mailto:horizons@hackclub.com) and we'd love to answer questions or hop on a call.

## What is Hack Club's child protection policy?

Hack Club takes the safety and well-being of all participants seriously. You can read our full [safeguarding policy](https://hackclub.com/safeguarding-policy) to learn about our commitment to child protection.

## I'm not good at coding / I've never made projects before. Can I still participate?

Yes, we want you to! This hackathon is for all skill levels. We'll be running workshops and support calls to help everyone learn together.

## What can I make?

Any kind of technical project is allowed - websites, games, hardware - anything!

## How do I qualify?
For Horizons Nexus (San Francisco), you are required to code and ship 30 hours of projects. Hour requirements for the other events are TBA, but will be around 30 hours.

## Can I use AI?

Use of generative AI **for graphics and audio** is **prohibited**. Use of generative AI for other purposes is allowed, but we will not accept projects which qualify as "AI slop." Additionally, you shouldn't write updates or project descriptions using AI.

We encourage you to write code without being completely reliant on AI, especially if you're a beginner! Writing code yourself is the best way to learn, and learning the fundamentals allows you to become a more technical person and build better projects - even with the use of AI tools.

"AI slop" are projects which feel like they have little human effort put in beyond vibecoding for the sake of making any project at all to code for hours. Examples include projects like "interactive periodic table" and "generic task management website." We reserve the right to decide whether or not a project qualifies as "AI slop" and reject it. If your project is polished and looks like it has a real human touch, we're highly unlikely to reject it!

## Does making art or music count towards my time?

Yes; artists and musicians are encouraged to participate even if you've never coded before. A maximum of ⅓ of your total time time logged can be spent on music or art. You can use [Lapse](https://lapse.hackclub.com) to log your hours on art!

## Can I work in a team?

Yes! Teamwork is heavily encouraged. You must log your work separately from your teammates, and art or music can make up a maximum of ⅓ of the time spent on the project.

## How many projects can I make while qualifying?

You can make as many as you want! Projects will only count towards your progress when they're in a usable state – they don't need to be finished, but anything you make should be able to be called a 'demo' by the time you ship it.

## Can I submit an existing project?

Existing projects are allowed, but you can only submit time that was logged after February 22nd, 2026.

## Does my project need to be on GitHub?

All projects must be open source on GitHub or another similar software forge.

## Can I submit to this event and another Hack Club program?

Sorry, nope! "Double-dipping" is not allowed. If you make a project for this event, you can only submit it here, unless another program specifically states otherwise. (You can double-dip with [Sleepover](https://sleepover.hackclub.com)!)


## I'm 18, but I'm going to turn 19 by the end of this event. Can I still participate?

You can make projects, join AMAs, and win prizes before you turn 19, but you can only attend the in-person event if you're 18 or under at the time of the event.

## Can I get involved or contribute?

We love this question! We'd love more help running workshops, support calls, or even help organizing AMAs. If any of these interest you, let us know!
